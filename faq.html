About Sealcoating

Sealcoating is a protective layer applied to asphalt pavement that shields it from damaging elements like UV rays, water, oil, and chemical spills. It acts like sunscreen for your driveway, preventing oxidation and extending the life of your pavement. Regular sealcoating can double the lifespan of your asphalt and save you thousands in replacement costs.

We recommend sealcoating every 2-3 years for optimal protection. However, the frequency depends on factors like traffic volume, sun exposure, and the condition of your pavement. New driveways should wait at least 6 months before their first sealcoating to allow the asphalt to cure properly.

Sealcoating typically takes 24-48 hours to fully cure, depending on weather conditions. We recommend keeping vehicles off the surface for at least 24 hours, and foot traffic should be avoided for 4-6 hours. Warm, sunny weather speeds up drying time, while cool or humid conditions may require longer curing.

The ideal sealcoating season in Connecticut is late spring through early fall, when temperatures are consistently above 50°F. We need at least 24 hours of dry weather for proper curing. Our busy season runs from May through October.

About Crack Filling

Cracks allow water to penetrate beneath your pavement. In Connecticut's freeze-thaw climate, this water expands when frozen, causing cracks to grow larger and eventually leading to potholes and structural damage. Filling cracks early is the most cost-effective way to prevent expensive repairs.

We use commercial-grade hot-applied rubberized crack sealant. Unlike cold-pour products from hardware stores, our hot rubber remains flexible through temperature changes, creating a lasting seal that moves with your pavement.
